# Phase 08.1 Plan 01 Summary

**The live charting screen now uses a compact utility bar, dominant left-side zone workspace, right-side control stack, and hidden pitch-history reveal**

## Accomplishments
- Rebuilt `LiveChartingView.swift` around a top utility bar plus a two-column workspace, with the zone canvas expanded on the left and operational controls stacked on the right.
- Removed permanent pitch history from the primary screen and moved it into a secondary sheet surfaced from the utility bar.
- Tightened the zone workspace so the Trackman grid scales larger and wastes less space on the main charting surface.

## Decisions Made
- Chose a hidden history sheet instead of a persistent card so the main charting canvas and control stack stay focused on the current pitch.
- Kept matchup, inning, outs, mode switching, and history access in one compact utility bar to avoid fragmenting global context across multiple cards.

## Next Phase Readiness
- The shell now has stable space for mode-specific controls and a confirm strip without collapsing back into stacked bottom rows.
- The right-side control stack can now absorb mode setup, totals, and confirm-first pitch entry without another layout rewrite.
